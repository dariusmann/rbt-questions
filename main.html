As an RBT, how would you implement a stimulus control transfer procedure to train a new behavior?

Options:

  • A: By frequently alternating between known and unknown instructions
  • B: By providing a reinforcer regardless of the client's response
  • C: By providing a verbal instruction without any visual or physical guidance
  • D: By solely relying on negative reinforcement

Correct Answer: A - By frequently alternating between known and unknown instructions

  • A: This is the correct answer. Stimulus control transfer procedures involve teaching the learner to respond to new stimuli (unknown instructions) by initially discriminating them from known stimuli and gradually fading in the new stimuli while fading out the known stimuli.
  • B: This is incorrect. Providing a reinforcer regardless of the client's response can reinforce both correct and incorrect responses and does not inherently lead to effective behavior change.
  • C: This is incorrect. While verbal instruction can be beneficial, it alone does not constitute a stimulus control transfer procedure. Visual or physical guidance, along with reinforcement, are typically required to train new behaviors.
  • D: This is incorrect. While negative reinforcement can play a role in behavior change, solely relying on it does not constitute a stimulus control transfer procedure.
